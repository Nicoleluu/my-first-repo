About Me

I graduated from Rhode Island School of Design (RISD) in Industrial Design and am currently studying Computational Design Practices (CDP) at Columbia GSAPP. My background is rooted in product design, but my interests have gradually expanded toward systems, interaction, and computational workflows.

Why CDP

I joined CDP because I wanted to expand my design practice into the computational field. Coming from an industrial design background, I became increasingly interested in how computation can shape systems, behaviors, and environments beyond the scale of individual objects. CDP allows me to explore these ideas through code, data, and interactive systems.
